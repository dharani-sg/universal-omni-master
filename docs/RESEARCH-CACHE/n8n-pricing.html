When you start a free trial on the Business plan, you’ll get full access to all Business features for 14 days.

No credit card is required for Starter and Pro trials. A credit card is only required for the Business plan free trial.

Executions are much more powerful than the ‘operations’ or ‘tasks’ that you’re billed on in other products.

An execution is a single run of your entire workflow. It doesn’t matter how many steps are in the workflow or how much data it processes — it’s still a single execution.

This makes your usage more predictable than other tools where you pay individually for every step or task. Plus n8n workflows you can do things in a single execution that would take 10,000 operations in other tools.

AI Assistant (currently in early preview) is included in all n8n Cloud plans. Each plan comes with a monthly allowance of AI credits: 2,300 on Starter and 5,700 or 13,700 on Pro, depending on your plan size. Free trials include 800 credits, which expire when the trial ends.

Credits are consumed as you use AI Assistant. How much depends on the size and complexity of what you're building. Your balance refreshes every month, and unused credits don't roll over. It's not yet possible to buy additional credits, so if you regularly need more, upgrading to a higher plan is the way to get a larger allowance.

On self-hosted n8n, AI Assistant is coming soon. You'll bring your own API key, so no credit limits apply.

If you're already using n8n, you can assess your weekly or monthly usage by reviewing your instance's execution logs.If you're not yet using n8n, you can estimate your usage by listing your main use cases and evaluating how often each one will run. Here are a few examples:

• Scheduled workflows: For workflows triggered on a regular schedule, count how many times the schedule runs in a month. For instance, a daily schedule would result in 30 or 31 executions per month, while one that runs every 5 minutes would result in about 8,600–8,900 executions monthly.

• Webhook-based workflows: Estimate how many times the event that triggers your webhook occurs per day. That number equals your daily executions.

• Chatbot use case: Estimate how many conversations the chatbot will handle each week, based on your user base and expected frequency of interaction. Then multiply that by the average number of messages per conversation. This gives you the total weekly executions for the chatbot.

We keep your most recent executions so you can inspect and debug them. Each plan has a limit on the number of saved executions, a limit on the total storage they use (in Cloud), and a retention window in days. We keep executions until you reach the first of those, then remove the oldest. Reaching a limit does not stop your workflows from running. It only changes how much history is retained.
